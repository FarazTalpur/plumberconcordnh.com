Frequently Asked Questions

A greywater recycling system collects wastewater from sources such as sinks, showers, and washing machines, then treats and reuses it for non-potable purposes like irrigation and toilet flushing.

Greywater is wastewater from sources like baths, sinks, and washing machines that can be recycled for non-drinking purposes. Blackwater is wastewater from toilets that contains human waste and requires more extensive treatment to recycle.

Using a greywater recycling system can reduce water usage, lower water bills, conserve water resources, and promote a more sustainable household or building.

Yes, when properly treated and filtered, greywater can be safely reused for specific purposes like irrigation and toilet flushing. However, it should not be used for drinking or cooking.

Regular maintenance for a greywater recycling system includes cleaning filters, checking pumps and connections, inspecting storage tanks, and ensuring the system is operating efficiently to prevent blockages and odors.
