Frequently Asked Questions

Bathroom drains can become clogged due to hair, soap scum, toothpaste, dirt, and other debris that accumulates over time. Hard water minerals can also contribute by narrowing the pipes.

It's advisable to clean your bathroom drain once a month to prevent buildup and keep the water flowing freely. Regular cleaning can prevent the need for more extensive, professional cleaning services.

The safest way to clean a bathroom drain is by using a mixture of baking soda and vinegar. Pour half a cup of baking soda followed by half a cup of vinegar down the drain, let it sit for 15-30 minutes, and then flush it with hot water.

While chemical drain cleaners can be effective, they can also damage your pipes and are harmful to the environment. Use them sparingly, and opt for natural solutions or mechanical methods like a plunger or drain snake when possible.

If your bathroom drain remains clogged after cleaning, try using a plunger or a drain snake. If the problem persists, it may be necessary to call a professional plumber to address potential underlying issues.
