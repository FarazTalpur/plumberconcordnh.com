Frequently Asked Questions

It's recommended to have your sump pump inspected at least once a year, preferably before the rainy season starts. Regular inspection ensures it is functioning properly and can help prevent flooding.

Common signs include unusual noises, the pump running continuously, failure to start, foul odors, and water pooling around the pump. If you notice any of these issues, it's time to have your pump checked.

First, check the power supply and ensure the circuit breaker hasn't tripped. If everything seems fine but the pump still doesn't work, contact a professional plumber immediately to prevent basement flooding.

To prevent clogs, regularly clean the sump pit and the pump itself. Make sure the discharge pipes are clear and direct water away from the foundation. Installing a filter or cover can also help keep debris out of the sump pit.

The average lifespan of a sump pump is about 7 to 10 years. Regular maintenance and inspections can help extend the life of your pump, but it's advisable to start looking for a replacement around this time.
