Why Backflow Prevention is Critical

Backflow can occur due to changes in water pressure, often caused by natural disasters, fire hydrant use, or sudden stoppage in main water supply lines. These unwanted reversals present a significant risk by introducing harmful pollutants into the potable water system.

Frequently Asked Questions

Backflow is the undesirable reversal of water flow in a plumbing system, which can lead to contamination of the potable water supply with pollutants. It is a concern because it poses significant health risks to consumers and violates safety regulations.

Signs that your backflow device may need attention include unusual noises, leaks, or changes in water pressure. Regular testing by a certified professional is crucial to ensure proper functionality and compliance with local codes.

Typically, backflow prevention devices should be tested annually to ensure they are functioning correctly. However, local regulations may require more frequent testing depending on the risk level of the system or industry standards.

Backflow testing and repairs should be conducted by a certified backflow prevention assembly tester or a licensed plumber who has received specialized training in backflow preventers.

Failing to maintain backflow preventers can lead to water contamination, legal penalties, increased liability risks, and potential damage to the plumbing system. Regular maintenance is essential for safeguarding public health and adhering to regulations.
