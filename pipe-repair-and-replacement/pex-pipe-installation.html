Advantages of PEX Piping

Flexibility and Durability

  • Flexibility: PEX piping offers unmatched flexibility, which is particularly beneficial in the older infrastructures of Concord. Its ability to snake around corners and obstacles minimizes the need for joints and couplings.
  • Durability: Resistant to scale and chlorine, PEX piping performs exceptionally well under various environmental conditions. It is less susceptible to corrosion compared to traditional copper pipes, ensuring long-term reliability.

Cost-Effective Solutions

  • Reduced Labor Costs: The flexibility of PEX allows for quicker installations, leading to decreased labor times and costs.
  • Energy Efficiency: PEX retains heat better than metal pipes, which can result in lower energy costs, a crucial advantage for the residents of Concord, NH, during those harsh winter months.

Frequently Asked Questions

To install PEX pipes, you'll usually need a PEX cutter, crimp tool, go/no-go gauge, deburring tool, and expansion tool if using expansion-fitted connections. Additionally, pipe supports and hangers might be necessary for securing the PEX tubing.

PEX-A offers the most flexibility and is highly resistant to kinking, allowing it to be expanded and retracted with ease. PEX-B is slightly less flexible and uses a different manufacturing process that gives it improved chlorine resistance. PEX-C is the least flexible and is typically used in radiant floor heating applications.

Yes, PEX pipes can be used for hot water distribution. They are capable of withstanding temperatures up to 200Â°F (93Â°C) and are suitable for hot and cold water plumbing applications, as well as for underfloor heating systems.

To connect PEX pipes to copper pipes, you can use a push-fit connector, crimp-style fitting, or a soldered adapter. Push-fit connectors are the easiest option because they do not require any special tools or skills and can be attached by simply pushing the PEX and copper pipe ends into the fitting.

Although PEX pipes are more resistant to freezing than metal pipes, it is still advisable to insulate them, especially in cold climates or when running through unheated spaces. Insulation helps to improve energy efficiency and provide added protection against freezing.
